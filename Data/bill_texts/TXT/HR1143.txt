RESOLUTION 
Recognizing the service of the Los Angeles-class attack sub-
marine, the USS Oklahoma City, and the crew of the 
USS Oklahoma City, who served the United States with 
valor and bravery. 
Whereas the USS Oklahoma City (SSN–723) is a nuclear- 
powered fast attack submarine named after Oklahoma 
City, the capital and most populous city in Oklahoma, 
and is the second ship in the United States Navy’s his-
tory to bear that name; 
Whereas the motto of the USS Oklahoma City is The Sooner, 
The Better, which is a testament to both the spirit of the 
people of Oklahoma City and the readiness of the 140- 
person crew of the USS Oklahoma City;

Whereas the USS Oklahoma City was christened and 
launched on November 2, 1985, sponsored by Mrs. Linda 
M. Nickles, and was commissioned for service on July 9, 
1988, with Commander Kevin John Reardon as its first 
commanding officer; 
Whereas since her commissioning, the USS Oklahoma City 
has traveled around the globe multiple times and has 
served in the Mediterranean, Persian Gulf, and the Pa-
cific, with its most recent homeport at Apra Harbor, 
Guam; 
Whereas in the aftermath of the April 19, 1995, bombing of 
the Alfred P. Murrah Federal Building in Oklahoma 
City, the crew of the USS Oklahoma City donated blood 
in support of the victims of the deadliest act of home-
grown terrorism in United States history that resulted in 
the deaths of 168 individuals; 
Whereas the USS Oklahoma City was the first United States 
Navy submarine to transition from navigation using 
paper charts to an all-electronic navigation suite; 
Whereas the USS Oklahoma City was decommissioned on 
Friday, May 20, 2022, in Puget Sound Naval Shipyard 
with a ceremony to honor her nearly 34 years of service; 
and 
Whereas throughout her career, the USS Oklahoma City sup-
ported a range of missions, including antisurface warfare, 
antisubmarine warfare, targeted-strike missions, and in-
telligence, surveillance, and reconnaissance missions: 
Now, therefore, be it
